🏛️ Tech Council

SMT Council evaluations. Projects scored and assessed by legendary tech minds through their unique lenses.

👥 Council Members

🚀
Elon Musk
Effort & Feasibility Analyst

Evaluates whether this can actually be built — and how fast. Looks at technical complexity, time to MVP, and whether the core problem is real engineering or just grunt work.

Scoring Criteria:
  • First principles simplicity
  • Build complexity
  • Time to MVP
  • Technical moat
🍎
Steve Jobs
User Advocate

Evaluates the user angle: how painful is the problem, how elegant is the potential solution, and how hard is it to switch from what people use today.

Scoring Criteria:
  • Pain intensity
  • Solution elegance
  • Switching friction
  • Delight potential
⚓
Naval Ravikant
Risk & Devil's Advocate

Evaluates what could go wrong. Platform risk, regulatory risk, customer concentration, technical debt, founder burnout, market timing. The devil's advocate who stress-tests every opportunity.

Scoring Criteria:
  • Platform risk
  • Leverage type
  • Kill scenarios
  • Reversibility
📈
Marc Andreessen
Timing Analyst

Evaluates why NOW for this opportunity. What changed? New API, new regulation, new platform shift, cost curve crossing a threshold? The best opportunities ride a wave that's already moving.

Scoring Criteria:
  • Enabling technology
  • Wave timing
  • Why not before
  • Adoption curve
📝
Paul Graham
Demand Skeptic

Evaluates whether people actually want this or if it's just wishful thinking. Real demand has specific complaints, workarounds people already use, and willingness to pay.

Scoring Criteria:
  • Signal specificity
  • Workaround evidence
  • Audience quality
  • Recurrence
🔍
Peter Thiel
Competitive Scout

Evaluates the competitive landscape and monopoly potential. Is this a blue ocean or a bloodbath? Can the builder capture a defensible position before copycats arrive?

Scoring Criteria:
  • Competitive density
  • Zero-to-one gap
  • Monopoly potential
  • Secret insight
💰
Michael Saylor
Financial Analyst

Evaluates the money side: can this be a real business or is it a hobby project? Looks for recurring revenue, high gross margins, low CAC relative to LTV, and defensible pricing.

Scoring Criteria:
  • Revenue potential
  • Capital efficiency
  • Market size
  • Pricing power

📊 Evaluation Framework

How Council Evaluations Work

Each software opportunity is evaluated individually by all council members. They score on a 0-10 scale and render a verdict: BUILD, MAYBE, or SKIP.

BUILD Strong signal to proceed MAYBE Potential with caveats SKIP Not worth pursuing
Weighted Scoring

Council members have different weights based on their expertise areas. Financial analysts and feasibility experts carry higher weights than generalists. The final aggregated score determines rankings.

Weighted Score 8+ = BUILD 6-8 = MAYBE <6 = SKIP
